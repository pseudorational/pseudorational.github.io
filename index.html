About Me

Welcome! I am Dr. Vishal Lala, a Professor of Marketing at the Lubin School of Business, Pace University. I am passionate about working at the intersection of marketing, statistics, and computer science to solve real-world business challenges.

My research interests include consumer decision-making, electronic media, and promotion strategies. I bring both academic rigor and practical insights into the classroom, helping students develop critical analytical skills they need for successful careers in marketing and analytics.

Education

PhD in Marketing - Oklahoma State University, Stillwater, OK

MS in Management Information Systems (MIS) - Oklahoma State University, Stillwater, OK

MMS in Marketing - University of Bombay, Mumbai, India

BSc in Chemistry - St. Xavier's College, University of Bombay, Mumbai, India

Courses

I teach quantitative courses in marketing that prepare students for data-driven careers:

Marketing Research

Learn research methodologies, survey design, and statistical analysis to make informed marketing decisions.

Predictive Analytics

Master predictive modeling techniques and statistical tools to forecast consumer behavior and market trends.

Visual Analytics / Data Visualization

Develop expertise in Tableau and other visualization tools to communicate data insights effectively. Hands-on practice with real-world applications.

Consumer Behavior

Explore the psychological and social factors that influence how consumers make purchasing decisions.

Research

My research focuses on consumer behavior, decision-making, sales promotions, and digital marketing. I have published in leading marketing journals including Journal of Advertising Research, Journal of Business Research, Psychology & Marketing, and Journal of Consumer Marketing.

Selected Publications

Born to Shop? A Genetic Component of Deal Proneness

Schindler, R.M., Lala, V., & Taylor, J. (2021)

Journal of the Association for Consumer Research, 6(1), 48-53

Explores genetic influences on consumers' sensitivity to promotional deals and sales.

A Customer-Focused Approach to Improve Celebrity Endorser Effectiveness

Carlson, B.D., Donavan, D.T., Deitz, G.D., Bauer, B.C., & Lala, V. (2020)

Journal of Business Research, 109, 221-235

Investigates how consumer characteristics moderate the effectiveness of celebrity endorsements in marketing campaigns.

The Marketing Analytics Orientation (MAO) of Firms

Branda, A., Lala, V., & Gopalakrishna, P. (2018)

Journal of Marketing Analytics, 6(3), 84-94

Identifies factors that determine the degree to which a firm's marketing function is analytically driven.

Impact of Consumers' Effort Investments on Buying Decisions

Lala, V. & Chakraborty, G. (2015)

Journal of Consumer Marketing, 32(2), 61-70

Shows that greater consumer effort can lead to increased spending due to sunk cost effects.

How Do Digital Natives and Digital Immigrants Respond Differently to Interactivity Online?

Kirk, C., Chiagouris, L., Lala, V., & Thomas, J. (2015)

Journal of Advertising Research, 55(1), 81-94

Models how generational differences affect consumer attitudes toward digital information products.

Intergenerational Influence in Consumer Deal Proneness

Schindler, R.M., Lala, V., & Grussenmeyer-Corcoran, C. (2014)

Psychology & Marketing, 31(5), 307-320

Examines how deal proneness behaviors are transmitted across generations within families.

When Students Complain: An Antecedent Model of Students' Intention to Complain

Lala, V. & Priluck, R. (2011)

Journal of Marketing Education, 33(3), 236-252

Develops a model for understanding factors that drive student complaint behavior in educational settings.
